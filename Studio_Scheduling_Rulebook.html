Section 01 · Foundation

Universal Scheduling Rules

These rules govern all three locations without exception. A location-specific rule can add to these, but never contradict them.

Core Scheduling Principles

Barre 57 is the Studio's Backbone — Never Below 25% of Weekly Classes

Across all locations, Barre 57 constitutes 25–38% of all sessions. It must form the majority block of every weekly schedule. On any given day, a minimum of 2 Barre 57 slots must be filled on weekdays, and 1 on Sundays.

Three Time Bands Must Always Be Covered on Weekdays

Morning Band 07:00–09:30 | Midday Band 10:00–12:30 | Evening Band 17:30–20:00. All three bands must have at least one class every weekday at every location. Sundays may skip the Evening Band.

Saturday is Maximum-Intensity Day — Never Understaff It

Saturday is the highest or joint-highest session day across all three studios. Schedule the maximum number of Tier 1 trainers on Saturday. Never leave Saturday with a reduced schedule or Tier 3-only coverage.

Sunday is a Reduced Schedule — Afternoons First

Sunday consistently has the lowest volume (20–30% below peak days). Maximum 5–6 classes on Sunday. Favour the 10:00–12:00 and 16:00–18:30 windows. Do not start Sunday before 10:00 AM.

Only Qualified Trainers May Teach Specialist Formats

PowerCycle, Strength Lab, Pre/Post Natal, and Foundations are specialist formats. They must only be assigned to trainers with confirmed history in that class type. See the Trainer Profiles tab for per-trainer qualifications.

Express Classes Must Be Paired With Full-Length Equivalents on the Same Day

Barre 57 Express, Cardio Barre Express, Mat 57 Express, and PowerCycle Express are shortened variants (30–45 min). When scheduling any Express class, a full-length version of the same type must also run that day at another time slot.

Recovery is a Post-Intensity Class — Never the First Slot of the Day

Studio Recovery must always follow a high-energy class in the daily lineup. Best placed on weekend afternoons (12:30–16:00). Never schedule it as the opening class of any day.

Foundations Belongs in Off-Peak Slots

Foundations classes are beginner entry points. They should be placed in lower-demand times (early morning or mid-afternoon), never in the 11:30 or 19:15 peak slots which draw the most experienced attendees.

No Trainer Should Teach More Than 3 Back-to-Back Classes

Historical data shows max 4–5 classes per trainer per day, but quality drops beyond 3 consecutive. Build at least a 30-minute gap between any trainer's third consecutive class. Never schedule a trainer for more than 4 classes in one day.

Trainer Substitution Must Respect Class Certification

Step 1: Same tier, same class type. Step 2: One tier lower, same class type. Step 3: Cross-location trainer with ≥30 sessions at that location. Specialist classes (PowerCycle, Strength Lab, Pre/Post Natal) cannot be substituted with non-certified trainers under any circumstance.
